Economics

What if ignorance, not knowledge, is the economic object? When execution is automated and runs at near-zero marginal cost, the value-creating work moves upstream — to the layer where ignorance is shaped, classified, and allocated. This thread works out the economics of that shift.

Gopika Kannan & Dhruv Gupta

The epistemic economy Open research

The canonical knowledge-economy literature (Romer, Aghion, Acemoglu) treats knowledge as the non-rival productive factor and ignorance as residual friction. We invert the frame. Ignorance is the economic object; engineering it is the value-creating activity; knowledge is the settled residue of well-engineered ignorance, not the upstream input. In an economy where AI performs execution at near-zero marginal cost, the productive work that remains is deciding which problems are settled, which are not, what kind of unsettlement each exhibits, and what response it calls for.

The ignorance damage function

The framework's central move is an ignorance damage function, by direct structural analogy with Nordhaus. Nordhaus added a climate damage function to neoclassical growth theory and derived the welfare-positive case for carbon abatement. We add an ignorance damage function to growth theory and derive the welfare case for decision engineering. The apparatus carries across: a stock that accumulates from economic activity, a damage function tying the stock back to output, an optimal-control problem over the investment path. The difference is the result.

Decision Engineering Framework

The organising discipline. When execution is automated, the work that remains is judgment: recognising which kind of decision is in front of you, and staffing it with the kind of attention it actually requires. Decision engineering is the deliberate practice of (i) recognising which kind of decision is being made, (ii) staffing it with the human work it needs, and (iii) building the organisation around that recognition rather than around the legacy task structure.

Automation debt — working definition

The growing gap between an organisation's automated operational reach and its human capacity to govern, interrogate, and override that automation. It accrues silently as automation scales, and becomes visible only when an automated system fails.

AI deployment without judgment investment is not progress. It is accumulated risk, dressed up as productivity.

The role-shape inversion

Earlier firms had a wide base of execution work and a narrow apex of judgment. The automated firm inverts it: a narrow base of execution oversight and a broad apex of judgment and goal-setting. The change is structural, not gradual.

The economics of automated firms Working papers in progress

The formal economics, defended in standard apparatus. Automation debt is modelled as a negative off-balance-sheet cost function that accumulates with automation depth and the type-distribution of automated tasks — the mirror image of Brynjolfsson's positive intangible capital, and a third externality alongside the two already named in the literature (excessive automation; welfare-reducing new tasks).

Two results that travel as one-liners

The method-error floor. Perfect execution cannot remove method error — better execution only runs the wrong method more cleanly. Oversight is therefore not a startup cost to be relaxed once automation scales; its value rises with automation depth, because the same latent defect now propagates through more tasks, faster.

The residual-bearer identity. Any loss an automated process produces that is not charged to the project's own P&L must be named and assigned to whoever bears it — workers, users, regulators, the public. Off-balance-sheet does not mean no-one's-balance-sheet.

Worked example: a diagnostic at scale

At a prominent Asian electric-vehicle manufacturer, an uncertainty diagnostic was applied to a causal world-model of welded-subassembly failures. Rather than treat the diagnostic as one decision, the team classified the whole population of failure paths by what it would take to resolve each:

~60%resolvable from existing error codes (AI-amenable)
~12%need targeted additional evidence (moderate investment)
~28%structurally unresolvable without live data integration (a category change, not tuning)

The partition told the firm where to invest, and the recommendation differed in kind for each class. That is decision engineering in a manufacturing line: identify the cells, route attention by cell, invest in the upstream artefact that makes the routing possible.
