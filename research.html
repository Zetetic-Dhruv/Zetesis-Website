Research

What does it take for discovery to be reliable, transferable, and adaptable? We work this question on three fronts, each anchored in its own programme and feeding back into the others.

Φ
The calculus of inquiry

Epistemology

Ignorance as a first-class object with internal geometry, and inquiry as a controllable operator — imagine, experiment, describe, communicate — with verification keeping the model honest.

λ
Synthetic discovery

AI

Synthetic discovery as the centerpiece: process-level evaluation, a Goodhart-resistant intrinsic reward, machine-checked learning theory, and the transformer foundations beneath them.

∫
Ignorance as object

Economics

Automation debt, decision engineering, and an ignorance damage function for the economy where execution is automated and judgment is the scarce input.

The three threads are not parallel by accident. An epistemology that cannot be implemented stays decorative; an AI without a clear epistemology drifts; an economics without either is fiction.
