Research

What does it take for discovery to be reliable, transferable, and adaptable? We work this question on three fronts, each anchored in its own programme and feeding back into the others.

Φ
I · The calculus of inquiry

Epistemology

The agent-independent part. A calculus of inquiry — imagine, experiment, describe, communicate — treating ignorance as a first-class object with internal geometry, and verification as the operator that keeps a model honest.

→
λ
II · Synthetic discovery

AI

Synthetic discovery as the centerpiece: process-level evaluation, a Goodhart-resistant intrinsic reward, machine-checked learning theory, and the transformer foundations that make any of it carry weight in practice.

→
∫
III · Ignorance as object

Economics

Discovery, inquiry, and the structure of ignorance treated as economic objects. Automation debt, decision engineering, and an ignorance damage function for the AI-saturated economy.

→

The three threads are not parallel by accident. An epistemology that cannot be implemented stays decorative; an AI without a clear epistemology drifts; an economics without either is fiction.
